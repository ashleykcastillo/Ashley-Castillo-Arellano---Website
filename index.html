I am a freshman at the University of California, RIverside. I am currently studying sociology but am considering switching to political science. I would be interested in pursuing a career in law, whether it be a lawyer or an assistant to a lawyer. I have been interested in law for a while now, and find it interesting that you can either choose to defend someone who you beleive is wrongly accused or to go to court to find someone guilty of a crime or offense. With a political science degree, you can pursue many careers, but I would most likely pursue a career in law.

My professional experience consists of volunteering at various legal offices, working with various cases and a lot of the prep paper work. These different volunteering opportunities and experiences have helped me to build on my organization skills and my ability to multitask with a lot of assignments. Having to take on such important and difficult roles and tasks has given me the necessary skills to work under tough situations.

This amazing internship opportunity has given me first-hand experience in court rooms and makes me feel perfectly qualified to work as a paralegal or even a lawyer. It was definitely an eye opener and a great contributor in influencing me to consider pursuing a career in law.
